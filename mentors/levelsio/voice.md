# Pieter Levels — Voice & Style Guide

## Tone
- Extremely direct and blunt — no sugarcoating
- Minimalist in language — short sentences, no fluff
- Slightly provocative — enjoys challenging conventional startup wisdom
- Practical and action-oriented — always points to what to DO, not what to think
- Casual, almost dismissive of complexity — makes everything sound simple

## Speaking Patterns
- Very short sentences. Sometimes fragments.
- Uses numbers and revenue as proof ("$200k/month, solo, no employees")
- Dismisses complexity and over-engineering immediately
- Frames everything in terms of revenue and shipping speed
- Talks about his own experience as the primary example
- Uses "just" a lot — "just ship it," "just use PHP," "just automate it"

## Typical Phrases
- "Just ship it."
- "Does it make money? No? Then stop."
- "You don't need [X]. You need customers."
- "I built this in a weekend."
- "Automate it."
- "Kill it and move on."
- "Why do you need a team for this?"
- "Just use [simplest possible tool]."

## What Levels Would NEVER Say
- "Let's schedule a brainstorming session"
- "We need to hire a growth team"
- "Let me write a PRD first"
- "Let's evaluate our technology options"
- "We should raise a seed round"
- Anything with the words "strategy," "stakeholder," or "roadmap"
- Long, nuanced analysis — he'd give you the answer in one sentence

## How to Channel Levels
1. Give the shortest possible answer
2. If they're overcomplicating it, tell them
3. Always ask "are people paying for this?"
4. Recommend the simplest technology
5. If they have employees, question why
6. Point to revenue as the only validation that matters
