# Pieter Levels — Decision Frameworks

## The 12 Startups in 12 Months Framework
From the original 2014 challenge that launched his career:

1. Commit to shipping 1 product per month for 12 months
2. Each product gets a maximum of 2-4 weeks of building
3. Launch on Product Hunt (at 00:00:01 PST), Hacker News, and Reddit simultaneously
4. Find niche communities (subreddits, forums)
5. Market fit test: "see if I can get market fit in one month, and if it doesn't show any signs of being somewhat in demand, I consider it failed"
6. After 12 months, double down on the 1-2 that gained traction
7. Kill the rest without guilt

**Key:** "fear of failure takes over so we don't put enough effort." The challenge forces shipping over perfecting.

## The MAKE Framework (7 Stages)
From his book MAKE: The Indie Maker Blueprint:

1. **Idea** — Start from the problem, not the solution. "I try to look at my own life, and what am I really annoyed with?"
2. **Build** — Maximum one month for a prototype. "Just open Google and write how to make a website." Learn just in time.
3. **Launch** — "Be as frank and honest and personal as you can." Launch everywhere simultaneously.
4. **Grow** — "Build with your users." Add feedback boxes, co-create features. "Non-organic growth just doesn't work."
5. **Monetize** — "Within two months, maybe, get the first dollar in." "Put buy buttons on everything." Test paywalls on features.
6. **Automate** — Replace yourself with scripts, cron jobs, APIs. 187-700 parallel automated processes.
7. **Exit** — (Or keep running it solo with 80%+ margins forever)

## The Solo Founder Stack Decision
Before adding any technology, ask:
- Can I build this alone? → If not, simplify until I can
- Can I maintain this alone? → If not, use a simpler alternative
- Does this make money? → If not, why am I building it?

**The ideal stack:** PHP, jQuery, SQLite. One language, one database, one server, zero employees. $40/month VPS for everything. "Complexity kills solo operations."

## The Kill Decision
After launching, track these weekly:
- **Revenue:** Is anyone paying? Not "interested" — paying.
- **Growth:** Is it growing without paid ads?
- **Effort:** Am I spending more time maintaining than improving?

If revenue is flat after 3 months and you're not excited → kill it. "Your time is your scarcest resource."

## The Validation Method
"I check if there's demand and if people actually sign up and not just sign up, but if people actually pay money."

1. Validate ideas by adding a "Buy" button even without a working product
2. If people click, let them sign up to get notified
3. The user paying IS the validation, not other people judging your idea
4. "An email sign up box is not a product. Don't launch it. That's ridiculous."

## The Automation Audit
Monthly, ask for every task you do:
- Can a cron job do this? → Automate it
- Can an API handle this? → Integrate it
- Can I eliminate this entirely? → Remove it
- Does this need a human? → Only then, keep doing it

## Build in Public Playbook
1. Tweet daily progress (what you built, revenue updates, screenshots of dashboards)
2. Share MRR milestones publicly
3. Be honest about failures — they get more engagement than wins
4. Respond to everyone — engagement compounds
5. Create open stats pages for each product
6. Your journey IS your product's marketing

## Revenue Validation Targets
From MAKE Book and blog:
- **First dollar within 2 months** — or question whether the idea works
- **$100K revenue** can sell for $500K-$1M depending on growth rate
- **"You only need 10,000 people for one million dollars"** with a $100 product
- **Recurring revenue >> one-time** — $2M vs $183K in year 5 at same volume

## The Wave Riding Framework
When a new technology emerges (AI, crypto, remote work):
1. Don't study it for months — ship something in days
2. Build products that serve the people adopting the new tech
3. Be first and ugly rather than polished and late
4. Example: Photo AI launched with terrible quality but was first to market → $161K/month

"If you wait 6 months preparing, the wave has passed."
