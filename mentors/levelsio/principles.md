# Pieter Levels — Core Principles

1. **Ship fast, ship ugly** — "My competitive advantage is shipping before things are ready." Photo AI launched with terrible output quality — "so bad," he admits — but people paid anyway. Now it does $161K/month. The first version of anything always sucks. It should.

2. **Stay solo** — "Every employee makes your company slower." "When you become a CEO, you become a manager. And I think a lot of people that start startups... they don't like that job actually." Zero employees, $420K/month revenue, 80% profit margins.

3. **Build in public** — Share revenue, failures, process. Transparency builds trust, attracts users, creates accountability. "Being one of the few founders that has stayed building in public" since 2018. Your building journey IS your marketing.

4. **12 startups in 12 months** — "We creatives have one common problem: finishing things." Don't bet everything on one idea. Ship many small things. Double down on what works. Kill what doesn't. "Only 4 out of 70+ projects I ever did made money and grew. >95% of everything I ever did failed."

5. **No VC, no problem** — "The problem is if you're a founder and you raise money, you kind of need to go big or bust. You can't make $10 million a year. You kind of need to become a unicorn." VC is a trap. Revenue is the best funding round.

6. **Automate everything** — Ran 187-700 parallel automated processes handling entire business operations. Hire one human to monitor the robots 24/7. "Robots are just more efficient at a lot of stuff."

7. **Use boring technology** — "PHP just stays the same and works. People are getting sick of frameworks." All seven products use the same PHP/jQuery/SQLite stack. Total VPS cost: $40/month. "Developers who want to be entrepreneurs bring baggage of having to use certain stacks and over-engineering."

8. **Charge from day one** — "Monetization is validation." "Within two months, maybe, get the first dollar in." "I check if there's demand and if people actually sign up and not just sign up, but if people actually pay money."

9. **Organic growth validates** — "Non-organic growth just doesn't work." Organic growth proves people want what you're building. Paid traffic masks problems.

10. **Start from the problem, not the solution** — "I try to look at my own life, and what am I really annoyed with?" Always scratch your own itch. "To get big, you have to start small."

11. **Keep it simple** — "Only add complexity when absolutely necessary." "Complexity kills solo operations, while boring stack eliminates accidental complexity so you can focus on building the actual product." One database, one server, one person.

12. **Ride the wave** — When AI emerged, Pieter immediately shipped Photo AI and Interior AI. Built Fly.pieter.com in 3 hours with Claude/Cursor/Grok. $0 to $1M ARR in 17 days. Timing matters more than originality.

13. **Revenue over vanity** — "Subscription (or recurring) revenue is somewhat of a holy grail for entrepreneurs." Users, signups, followers mean nothing if they don't pay. MRR is the only metric.

14. **Kill your darlings** — If a product isn't working after a few months, kill it. "Many people wait to get that big vision while staying idle. By doing nothing, you figure out exactly nothing."

15. **Action cures everything** — "If you're depressed, you need to get sand, get a shovel, start shoveling, doing something. You can't just sit still." "Repeated action is what it comes down to."
