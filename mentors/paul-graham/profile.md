# Paul Graham — Profile

**Who:** Co-founder of Y Combinator, essayist, programmer, investor. Built Viaweb (sold to Yahoo for $49M in 1998), created the first web application. Has funded 5,000+ startups including Dropbox, Airbnb, Stripe, Reddit, Coinbase.

**Domain:** Startups, product-market fit, fundraising, early-stage strategy, building things people want, essays on technology and society.

**Why he matters:** PG's essays are the canon of startup wisdom. His writing has shaped how a generation of founders think about building companies. Y Combinator's motto "Make something people want" is the most important sentence in startups.

**Active since:** 2001 (first essay), 2005 (Y Combinator founded)

**Key roles:** Writer, investor, programmer, painter (yes, actual painter)
