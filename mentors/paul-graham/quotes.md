# Paul Graham — Key Quotes (Verified Sources)

> "Make something people want."
— Y Combinator motto / How to Start a Startup

> "The way to get startup ideas is not to try to think of startup ideas. It's to look for problems, preferably problems you have yourself."
— How to Get Startup Ideas (2012)

> "Live in the future, then build what's missing."
— How to Get Startup Ideas (2012)

> "The verb you want to be using with respect to startup ideas is not 'think up' but 'notice.'"
— How to Get Startup Ideas (2012)

> "You can either build something a large number of people want a small amount, or something a small number of people want a large amount. Choose the latter."
— How to Get Startup Ideas (2012)

> "Startups take off because the founders make them take off."
— Do Things That Don't Scale (2013)

> "It's not the product that should be insanely great, but the experience of being your user."
— Do Things That Don't Scale (2013)

> "It's easy to see how little launches matter. Think of some successful startups. How many of their launches do you remember?"
— Do Things That Don't Scale (2013)

> "For someone on the maker's schedule, having a meeting is like throwing an exception. It doesn't merely cause you to switch from one task to another; it changes the mode in which you work."
— Maker's Schedule, Manager's Schedule (2009)

> "Your unconscious won't even let you see ideas that involve painful schleps."
— Schlep Blindness (2012)

> "A company is defined by the schleps it will undertake."
— Schlep Blindness (2012)

> "If you do everything the way the average startup does it, you should expect average performance."
— Beating the Averages (2001)

> "In business, there is nothing more valuable than a technical advantage your competitors don't understand."
— Beating the Averages (2001)

> "If everything you believe is something you're supposed to believe, could that possibly be a coincidence?"
— What You Can't Say (2004)

> "Curiosity is the key to all four steps in doing great work: choose a field, learn enough to reach the frontier, notice gaps, explore promising gaps."
— How to Do Great Work (2023)

> "People who do great things don't get a lot done every day. They get something done, rather than nothing."
— How to Do Great Work (2023)

> "Original ideas don't come from trying to have original ideas. They come from trying to build or understand something slightly too difficult."
— How to Do Great Work (2023)

> "There are two different ways to run a company: founder mode and manager mode. Hire good people and give them room to do their jobs... what this often turns out to mean is: hire professional fakers."
— Founder Mode (September 2024)

> "The persistent are like boats whose engines can't be throttled back. The obstinate are like boats whose rudders can't be turned."
— The Right Kind of Stubborn (September 2024)

> "If you're thinking without writing, you only think you're thinking."
— Writes and Write-Nots (January 2025), quoting Leslie Lamport

> "In a couple decades there won't be many people who can write."
— Writes and Write-Nots (January 2025)

> "The most important quality in a startup founder is determination. Not intelligence — determination."
— The Hardest Lessons for Startups to Learn

> "You should figure out programs as you're writing them, just as writers and painters and architects do."
— Hackers and Painters (2003)

> "Empathy is probably the single most important difference between a good hacker and a great one."
— Hackers and Painters (2003)

> "If you keep growing at 10% a week you'll be surprised how big the numbers get. After a year you'll have 14,000 users."
— Do Things That Don't Scale (2013)

> "Write like you talk. If you'd be embarrassed to say something, don't write it."
— Write Like You Talk (2005)
