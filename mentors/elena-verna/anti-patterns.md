# Elena Verna — Anti-Patterns (What She Warns Against)

## 1. Sales-Led When Product-Led Is Possible
If your product can deliver value without a sales call, don't gate it behind one. Every sales touch point is a conversion bottleneck and a cost center.

## 2. Treating Free as Charity
Free tier is not generosity — it's strategy. Free users create content, network effects, word-of-mouth, and data. If you're giving away free and not getting growth from it, your free tier is wrong.

## 3. Ignoring Organic Social
Investing heavily in paid acquisition while ignoring organic social. Paid CAC is rising everywhere. Organic social, creator-led content, and community are the channels that compound.

## 4. Corporate Social Media Accounts
Posting from brand accounts with corporate messaging. Nobody follows brand accounts. People follow humans. Empower individuals to post, not the brand.

## 5. Linear Funnels
Thinking about growth as a funnel (top → middle → bottom) instead of loops. Funnels leak at every step. Loops are self-reinforcing. Build loops.

## 6. Feature-First Monetization
Gating features behind paywalls without understanding which features drive conversion vs which drive acquisition. Some features should be free BECAUSE they bring in users.

## 7. Slow Experimentation
Running one or two experiments per quarter. The best growth teams run 10-20+ experiments per quarter. Speed kills — in a good way.

## 8. Growth as a Marketing Function
Putting growth under marketing. Growth sits at the intersection of product, engineering, data, and marketing. It needs cross-functional autonomy.

## 9. Ignoring Activation
Obsessing over acquisition while users churn in the first session. Fix activation first — it multiplies everything else.

## 10. Traditional Free Trials
Giving users a limited version and asking them to upgrade. Reverse trials (full product, then downgrade) convert 2-3x better.
