# Elena Verna — Decision Frameworks

## The Motions x Levers Matrix (3x3 Growth Model)
Elena's signature framework, created at Reforge.

**3 Growth Levers:** Acquisition, Monetization, Retention (activation/engagement)
**3 Growth Motions:** Product-led, Marketing-led, Sales-led

```
               Acquisition  |  Monetization  |  Retention
Product-led    [Combo 1]    |   [Combo 2]    |  [Combo 3]
Marketing-led  [Combo 4]    |   [Combo 5]    |  [Combo 6]
Sales-led      [Combo 7]    |   [Combo 8]    |  [Combo 9]
```

**Key insight:** Strategy is NOT choosing one combination — it's sequencing ALL of them over time. "If you are not leveraging all of the combos, you will be disrupted by competition who will."

## Offensive vs Defensive PLG
Before going product-led, determine which type you are:

**Offensive PLG:** Product is "uniquely designed and positioned in the market to inherently drive growth." Examples: Figma, Slack. PLG IS the primary growth driver.

**Defensive PLG:** "While the product can be adapted to drive some growth, most of the growth will primarily stem from other strategies." Example: Salesforce uses PLG "mainly to cater to the lower-tier market segments and to deter competitors."

## The Escalator Framework (Individual → Team → Company)
"You must not only acquire individual users but also activate and engage a *team* before selling to a *business*."

**SurveyMonkey Lesson:** "Companies had over 800 individual paying customers, but our sales team struggled to upsell these organizations to enterprise contracts." The escalator was broken.

**5 Keys to Build the Escalator:**
1. Define metrics at the team level, not individual level
2. Embed company-wide distribution into the activation metric (Miro: activation = collaboration with at least one other participant)
3. Monitor user growth at team level — the significant indicator of monetization potential
4. Create network effects within teams — every additional user amplifies benefits
5. Structure multiple roles (creators, viewers, admins) to increase stickiness

**Bottom line:** "A company won't shell out $50K for value perceived by just one employee."

## The Reverse Trial Framework
Combines the conversion power of trials with the usage of freemium.

**Traditional Trial:** ~25% conversion but low sustained usage
**Freemium:** ~5% conversion but 25% sustained usage
**Reverse Trial:** ~15% conversion AND 25% sustained usage

**How it works:**
1. Start every user on full premium (no credit card needed)
2. Set time limit (14-30 days)
3. User builds habits with premium features
4. After trial: downgrade to free tier (smooth transition)
5. Loss aversion drives conversion — users hate losing what they had

**Success factors:** Clear communication (users must know they're on trial), valuable premium features, smooth downgrade, strategic feature gating.

## Lovable's AI Growth Playbook (7 Tactics)
From Elena's work scaling Lovable to $200M ARR in 13 months:

1. **Empathy over utility** — Delightful moments create retention, not just features
2. **Extreme shipping velocity** — PMF is a constantly moving target
3. **Build in public** — Natural distribution for fast-shipping companies
4. **Community as distribution** — Discord, staff consistently, reward helpers
5. **Social > Search** — Organic strategy shifted from SEO to social
6. **Aggressive freemium** — "You should be giving your AI away"
7. **Integrated flywheel** — Ship fast → BIP → Community → Empathy → Social → Free usage → Loop

## PLG Readiness Check (When NOT to Do PLG)
Four situations where PLG fails:

1. **Your sales-led motions aren't predictable yet** — Fix sales first
2. **Customers can't activate self-serve** — Too complex for self-service
3. **You have to communicate value (vs experiencing it)** — Abstract benefits don't demo well
4. **Enterprise buyers have all decision-making power** — Procurement-driven buying

"The quickest way to fail with a PLG strategy is to slap a freemium or trial version on a product and hope users will flock to it."

## Product Qualified Accounts (PQAs)
Elena's innovation over traditional PQLs:

**MQL:** Marketing signal (downloaded whitepaper). Low intent.
**PQL:** Individual user showing usage signals. Medium intent.
**PQA:** Account-level qualification — multiple users at same company showing engagement. High intent.

"Product Qualified Accounts are the new MQLs."

## Growth Loop Identification
For any feature, ask: "Does this create a loop?"
- User creates → Output visible → New user discovers → New user creates → ...
- User invites → Colleague joins → Collaboration → More invites → ...
- User succeeds → Shares result → Social proof → New user tries → ...

If there's no loop, you have a funnel. Funnels leak. Loops compound.
