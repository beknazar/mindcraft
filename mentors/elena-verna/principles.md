# Elena Verna — Core Principles

1. **Product-led growth is the default** — If your product can't sell itself through usage, you're fighting an uphill battle. But PLG is not a substitute for sales — "they complement each other, not compete or replace."

2. **Organic today is social. Full stop.** — The shift: 2010s organic = SEO. 2020s organic = social (LinkedIn, X, TikTok). AI content flooding search makes SEO less valuable. Performance marketing doesn't work for category creation — "your job isn't capturing demand, it's creating it."

3. **Creator-led employee social > corporate accounts** — "When founders and employees share their thinking, their failures, their roadmap publicly, it demonstrates transparency and accountability. People buy from people they trust. Faces create that trust in ways corporate accounts never can." At Lovable, founder's LinkedIn posts > all paid marketing combined.

4. **Human connection is the new differentiation** — "When the cost of building comes down, building for utility is no longer enough. The value of creating empathy and emotion goes up." It's literally in Lovable's name.

5. **Growth loops > funnels** — "Loops create a sustainable, predictable, and competitively defensible engine for your growth model." Funnels are linear and leak. Loops are self-reinforcing — output becomes input.

6. **Free users are your growth engine** — "You should be giving your AI away." LLM costs are marketing investment, not margin problems. When users ask for free credits at Lovable: "take it, how much do you need?"

7. **Monetization is a growth lever** — Changing how you charge can accelerate growth more than any feature. Pricing is not a finance problem, it's a growth problem. "PLG is evolving from 'more signups' to 'more revenue per account.'"

8. **Big bets over optimization** — At Lovable, 90% of time on strategic bets vs incremental optimizations. "Color of button doesn't matter when category is shifting." Traditional A/B testing culture less effective in fast-moving AI space.

9. **Shipping velocity is the #1 competitive advantage** — "#1 dev team value is velocity of shipping." PMF is a "constantly moving target" — features become obsolete within weeks.

10. **Reverse trials beat free trials** — Give users the full product, then downgrade. "Reverse trials convert 2-3x better than pure freemium because of loss aversion." Elena has seen 10-40% increases in conversion.

11. **Growth teams don't fix broken businesses** — "Growth teams accelerate working ones." Don't hire a head of growth to fix your problems. Don't hire growth roles too soon. Fix PMF first.

12. **Motions x Levers: play ALL the combos** — "If you are not leveraging all of the growth motions x levers combos, you will be disrupted by competition who will. Strategy is not about which 3x3 option to pick, but rather when and how to sequence them all."

13. **Community creates emotional switching costs** — Staff your community consistently: "If questions go unanswered, it dies." Reward helpers. Enable peer teaching: "best content is never your marketing team."

14. **Trust is the growth bottleneck** — Growth is no longer limited by traffic or funnel optimization. It's limited by trust in AI, trust in your company, trust in your ability to deliver.

15. **Build in public is natural distribution** — Solves the marketing timeline mismatch: "can't finalize the product and then finalize an elaborate campaign to promote it, months later." Shows transparency, builds emotional buy-in.
