# Example: Strategy Mode

## Input
```
/mindcraft strategy: we have 2k users but growth is flat for the last 3 months
```

## Output

### The Roundtable

---

**Paul Graham opens:**

Flat growth at 2K users means one of two things: either you've saturated your initial channel, or your product isn't retaining well enough to generate word-of-mouth.

Let me ask the harder question: of those 2K users, how many would be genuinely upset if your product disappeared tomorrow? If it's less than 40% — you have a product problem, not a growth problem. Go talk to your most active users this week. Not a survey — actual conversations. Ask them what they'd miss, what's frustrating, what they wish it did. The answer to your growth problem is in those conversations.

If retention is strong (40%+ would be upset), then your acquisition channel is exhausted. You launched on Product Hunt, got some HN traffic, and that's dried up. You need a new channel. But here's the key: the right channel depends on who your happiest users are and how they describe your product. Listen to their words — that's your marketing copy.

---

**Elena Verna responds:**

PG is right about retention — fix that first. But I want to dig into the growth mechanics.

At 2K users, you should be seeing some organic growth if the product has value. The fact that you're flat means one of these is broken:

1. **No growth loop** — Users come in, use the product, but nothing in their usage creates new user acquisition. Is there a sharing moment? A collaboration feature? Anything that naturally puts your product in front of non-users?

2. **Activation is broken** — You might be acquiring users who never reach the "aha moment." Check your activation rate. What percentage of signups do the core action within the first session? If it's under 30%, that's your problem.

3. **No word-of-mouth trigger** — What would make a user screenshot your product and post it? If nothing, you need to create that moment.

My recommendation: build a "made with [your product]" badge or output that users share naturally. This is how Figma, Notion, and Lovable grow. The output IS the marketing.

---

**Pieter Levels pushes back:**

2K users and flat? Here's what I'd do: stop everything and look at revenue.

Are these 2K users paying? If not, you don't have 2K users — you have 2K free accounts. That's nothing. Charge them. Add a $9/month plan tomorrow. See how many stay. The ones who pay are your real users. Build for them.

If they ARE paying and growth is flat, launch a new product. Seriously. Take what you've learned, build something adjacent in 2 weeks, and see if it grows faster. Don't spend 6 months trying to squeeze growth out of a product that might have a natural ceiling.

I had this with 3 of my projects. Flat at a few thousand users. I killed them and built Photo AI instead. Now it does $100K/month. Sometimes the answer isn't "fix growth" — it's "build the next thing."

Also — are you tweeting about this? Building in public? If your 2K users don't see you actively building, they forget about you. Tweet every day. Ship updates visibly.

---

**Elena responds to Levels:**

I disagree about killing the product at 2K users — that's premature. But Pieter's point about charging is valid. Free-to-paid conversion is the ultimate validation test.

**PG adds:**

Pieter's right that you should charge if you aren't already. But I wouldn't kill the product yet. 2K users is actually a lot if they're the right users. The question is whether you've found a small group that LOVES it, or a large group that's lukewarm. A small group of lovers is the foundation. A large group of lukewarm users is a warning sign.

---

### Synthesis & Action Plan

**Priority 1 (This week):** Talk to 10 of your most active users. Ask: "Would you be upset if we shut down? What do you use us for? Who else should use this?"

**Priority 2 (Next 2 weeks):** Fix activation. Measure what % of signups complete the core action in session 1. If under 30%, simplify the onboarding until it's above 40%.

**Priority 3 (Month 1):** Create a sharing moment. Build a feature where user output is visible to non-users (public pages, "made with" badges, embeddable widgets).

**Priority 4 (Ongoing):** Build in public. Tweet daily updates, revenue, learnings. Your journey is your marketing at this stage.
