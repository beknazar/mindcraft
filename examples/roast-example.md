# Example: Roast Mode

## Input
```
/mindcraft roast "We're building an AI-powered CRM for SMBs with a freemium model"
```

## Output

### Paul Graham
Look, I see this idea show up at YC every batch. "AI-powered [existing category] for [segment]." It's a tar pit.

Here's the problem: CRM is a solved market. Salesforce, HubSpot, Pipedrive — they have thousands of engineers, decades of integrations, and massive network effects. You're proposing to compete with them by adding "AI" to the label.

What *specifically* does your AI do that's so good that an SMB would rip out their existing CRM? "AI-powered" is not an answer. That's a feature, not a product. And features get copied in weeks.

**The real question:** What problem do SMBs have that NO existing CRM solves, where AI is the only way to solve it?

---

### Elena Verna
Your freemium model is probably wrong.

SMBs don't discover CRMs through product-led growth — they google "best CRM for small business" and pick one from a listicle. Your growth loop doesn't exist. There's no viral mechanic in a CRM. User A using your CRM does not naturally lead to User B discovering it.

Where's your acquisition strategy? If you say "content marketing and SEO," you're competing with HubSpot's $700M/year marketing machine. Good luck.

**What I'd change:** Find a niche where the CRM IS the collaboration tool — where sharing IS the product. Real estate agents sharing listings? Recruiters sharing candidates? That's a growth loop.

---

### Pieter Levels
Why are you building a CRM? Just use the ones that exist.

Serious question: have 10 people told you "I would pay $X/month for this"? Not "that sounds cool" — actually committed money?

Also "AI-powered" means nothing. Every product is "AI-powered" now. That's like saying your car has wheels.

If you're going to build this, here's what I'd do: ship a landing page today, charge $29/month, see if anyone signs up. If nobody pays in 2 weeks, kill it and build something else.

---

### Synthesis

**All three agree on:**
1. "AI-powered CRM" is not differentiated enough
2. The freemium model lacks a growth loop
3. The market is dominated by well-funded incumbents

**Top 3 action items:**
1. **Find the wedge** — What specific workflow does your AI automate that's genuinely impossible without AI? Build only that, not a full CRM.
2. **Validate with money** — Get 10 SMBs to commit to paying before building. Not "interested" — paying.
3. **Find the loop** — Identify how one user's usage leads to another user's discovery. No loop = no PLG = you need a sales team = you need funding = you're competing with HubSpot.

**The single most important thing:** Stop building a CRM. Build the one AI-powered workflow that's 10x better than doing it manually, and let it grow from there.
