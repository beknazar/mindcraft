# Example: Advisory Mode

## Input
```
/mindcraft pg: I have 500 DAU on my dev tool. Should I add enterprise pricing?
```

## Output

**Paul Graham would say:**

No. You're at 500 DAU — that's nothing. You don't have product-market fit yet.

Here's the thing about enterprise pricing that nobody talks about: it's a distraction machine. The moment you add an enterprise tier, you'll start getting "enterprise" requests. Custom SSO. SOC 2 compliance. Dedicated support. SLAs. Each one sounds reasonable. Together, they'll consume your entire roadmap.

**Principles applied:**
- **#1: Make something people want** — At 500 DAU, you haven't proven this yet. Enterprise pricing won't help you find PMF faster.
- **#2: Do things that don't scale** — Talk to your 500 users individually. Why are they using your tool? What would make them use it 10x more?
- **#3: Launch fast, iterate** — Enterprise sales cycles are 6-12 months. You need feedback in days, not quarters.

**Key quote:**
> "If you have to choose between a mediocre founder with a great idea and a great founder with a mediocre idea, go with the great founder."

The same logic applies to your roadmap: choose between building what your 500 users desperately need vs. what hypothetical enterprise buyers might want. Always choose the desperate users.

**Contrarian pushback:** The one exception — if 3+ of your existing users are *already asking* to pay more for team features, that's organic pull. Follow the pull. But don't create it artificially.
